Literary analysis

The main goal of literary analysis is the appreciation of artistic craftsmanship. Looking at specific literary elements, you can analyze how an author explores some universal themes of the human experience.

For this assignment, you will analyze how setting and characterization are used to construct the theme(s) of a short story.

Theme

Themes are a huge part of what readers ultimately take away from a work of literature when they're done reading it. They're the universal lessons and ideas that we draw from our experiences of works of art.

Characterization

Characterization refers to the creation or construction of a fictional character. In explicit characterization, the author uses a narrator or the characters themselves to tell readers what a character is like. In implicit characterization, readers must infer for themselves what the character is like through the character's thoughts, actions, language, physical appearance, and interaction with other characters.

Setting

Setting refers to the time and place of a story and includes the atmosphere of specific locations, immediate and general social environments, as well as the time period and time span of the story.
